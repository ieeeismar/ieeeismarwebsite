We look forward to hosting you at **ISMAR 2026**, where we anticipate an engaging week of discussion on innovative research and advances in AR, VR, XR, and even reality-reality!

Below, we provide information regarding the registration fee structure and process. Please use the registration link above to complete your registration for ISMAR 2026.  


**Important Deadlines:**
  - **August 30, 2026**: Deadline for all authors and workshop organizers to register for inclusion of their papers, workshops, posters and demos in the conference program and proceedings. This deadline also applies to invited TVCG and CG&A talks.
  - **August 30, 2026**: Early registration deadline.



**Contacts for Questions:**
  - **Author/Submission Questions:** Contact ISMAR 2026 Program Chairs at [papers2026@ieeeismar.net](mailto:papers2026@ieeeismar.net).
  - **Visa Information and Registration Questions:** Contact the ISMAR 2026 Registration Chairs at [registration2026@ieeeismar.net](mailto:registration2026@ieeeismar.net).


> _Note: All deadlines are at 11:59 PM AoE on the stated date._

> _Note: All registration rates are listed in Euro (EUR) and include 22% VAT._

## New Registration Fee Model

For ISMAR 2026, we are proud to offer a new registration fee model.  We offer this model after significant thought and extensive conversations with peers and the ISMAR Steering Committee.

In short, there are two key aspects to the new registration fee model:
1. Student authors can register as an IEEE Student Member or Student Non-Member;

2. All registering authors can attach one contribution in their base registration and add more contributions for an additional fee(s) as needed.

More details about this new registration model are provided below.

## VISA Invitation Letters


> **Important:**  If you request a visa invitation letter, your registration cannot be cancelled after the visa letter has been issued unless the visa has been denied for any reason.


The official visa for Italy portal of the <a href="https://vistoperitalia.esteri.it/?lang=en_US" target="_blank">Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation</a> provides a guided procedure to verify visa requirements based on nationality, country of residence, reason for visit, and length of stay. If a visa is required, the portal provides the relevant information and forms needed to submit the application. Once the procedure is completed, if a visa invitation letter is necessary, please send a formal request to the ISMAR 2026 Registration Chairs at [registration2026@ieeeismar.net](mailto:registration2026@ieeeismar.net).


The Registration Chairs will reply with a template to be completed with all the information required to issue the visa support letter.


For more information, please visit the [Visa Information page](/2026/visa-information/).


**For Journal and Conference Paper Authors**

We strongly encourage authors to register immediately after receiving the final notification. Visa invitation letters will be issued as soon as possible. In any case, we advise authors to anticipate potential delays in the visa application process.

## Children’s Participation at ISMAR 2026

To foster a family-friendly environment, children of registered participants will be issued name tags and warmly welcomed to join the conference.

Participation in the banquet and meals is not guaranteed and will be provided upon availability.

## Cancellation Policy

All refund/cancellation requests must be received in writing at [registration2026@ieeeismar.net](mailto:registration2026@ieeeismar.net) by **September 27, 2026, 11:59 PM AoE**.

- Registration cancelled until September 27, 2026 will be refunded 80% of the registration fees.

- Registrations cancelled on or after September 28, 2026  will not be eligible for a refund.

**Notes:**

- Author registrations are non-refundable.
- Registrations for which a visa invitation letter has been issued will be refunded in full only if the visa application is denied for any reason and the refund request is submitted before September 27, 2026.

  <h2>FAQs</h2>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>When do I need to select "Full Conference Author" Registration?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          You must select Full Conference Author registration if you are an author of any accepted contribution
          (i.e., conference paper, journal paper, invited TVCG paper, invited CG&amp;A paper, workshop,
          workshop paper, poster, workshop poster, research demo or workshop demo) and no other co-author/co-organizer has yet
          to complete a Full Conference Author registration (and attached the shared contribution). At least
          one Author Registration per contribution is mandatory for inclusion in the conference
          program and/or proceedings.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>I am invited to present a previously published TVCG or CG&amp;A paper. Which registration category should I choose?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>You should register under Full Conference Author Registration.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What information is required when registering as an invited TVCG or CG&amp;A paper author?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>You must provide the full Paper DOI, for example:</li>
      </ul>

      <p>
        <code>10.1109/TVCG.20XX.XXXXXXX</code>
        (as listed in IEEE TVCG).
      </p>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Do Doctoral Consortium presenters need a Full Conference Author Registration?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          No. Full Conference Author Registration is not required if you are only presenting at the
          Doctoral Consortium.
        </li>

        <li>
          However, if a DC presenter has an accepted contribution – i.e., conference paper,
          journal paper, invited TVCG paper, workshop, poster, research demo, workshop
          paper, workshop poster, or workshop demo – at least one Full Conference Author Registration
          is required for that contribution to be included in the conference program
          and/or proceedings.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can I register multiple times?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>No. Each participant may complete only one registration.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>



  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What is the new registration fee model?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          The new model sets a base cost for student and non-student IEEE
          members/non-members that includes one contribution of any kind.
        </li>

        <li>
          Student contributors register as student members/non-members and pay
          a lower base cost. Non-student contributors register as regular
          members/non-members and pay a higher base cost.
        </li>

        <li>
          Each registration includes, at no additional cost, one contribution.
        </li>

        <li>
          Additional contributions can be added in an à la carte style.
        </li>

        <li>
          Certain restrictions apply to these additional contributions,
          concomitant with the overall goals of the new model
          (see below for more details).
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What are the goals of the new registration fee model?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <p>
        In short, our goals for this new approach are, in order of priority:
      </p>

      <ol>
        <li>
          <strong>Reduce costs for contributing students.</strong>
          Over the years, ISMAR (and other conferences) have moved to a model
          whereby every contributing author regardless of their career stage and contributions
          pay full conference, IEEE member/non-member fees. In these cases,
          a student presenting a poster would pay the same as a senior researcher
          presenting two papers.
        </li>

        <li>
          <strong>Encourage more student involvement,</strong> grow
          the student community, and establish long-lasting quality cohorts. This means
          creating cost structures that encourage faculty and career researchers
          to bring students to ISMAR.
        </li>

        <li>
          <strong>Allow registrants to add an arbitrary number of contributions</strong>
          to their registrations. This provides flexibility for students and
          faculty/career researchers alike. While we would prefer to see more
          student involvement, we know this is not possible for everyone in all cases.
        </li>
      </ol>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>How does the current registration fee model compare to the previous 1+1 model?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <p>
        To answer this question, assume that there exists a registration fee
        structure that employs the previous 1+1 registration model, and generates
        the equivalent income as the new model. Using these fees as a baseline,
        we can confidently state that the new model:
      </p>

      <ul>
        <li>Reduces costs for student-authors with one contribution by 21%.</li>
        <li>Keeps costs for student-authors with 2 contributions approximately the same.</li>
        <li>Keeps costs for non-student authors with one contribution approximately the same.</li>
        <li>Increases costs for non-student authors with two contributions by 35%.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What contributions must be attached to a registration?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          For contributions to be included in the IEEE Digital Library they
          must be attached to a valid Full Conference Author Registration (see below for who can register
          and the restrictions on adding additional contributions).
        </li>

        <li>
          Contributions that must be attached include: ISMAR TVCG papers, ISMAR
          conference papers, posters, demos, invited TVCG and invited CG&amp;A
          papers, workshops, workshop papers, workshop posters, and workshop demos.
        </li>

        <li>
          At least one workshop organizer must attach their workshop as a
          contribution to their registration.
        </li>

        <li>
          Contributions should be attached only to a single registration.
          Do not attach contributions to multiple registrations! This is confusing
          for the organizers and can lead to delays in registration processing.
        </li>

        <li>
          While attendees participating in Doctoral Consortium (DC) and Pitch Your
          Lab (PYL) must be registered to participate, these specific contributions
          (i.e., DC and PYL) do not need to be formally connected to a registration.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Who can register contributions?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <p>
        For contributions to be included in the IEEE Digital Library all
        contributions must be registered under one of the following
        registration categories:
      </p>

      <ul>
        <li>Student IEEE member/non-member full conference registration</li>
        <li>Non-student IEEE member/non-member full conference registration</li>
        <li>IEEE life member</li>
      </ul>

      <p>One-day passes cannot be used to register contributions.</p>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What restrictions apply when adding additional contributions?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          Non-students can add as many contributions as they wish as long as
          the registrant is one of the listed authors on each contribution registered.
        </li>

        <li>
          Students can add any contributions in which their name appears first
          in the author list (traditionally termed “first author”). Contributions
          where a student is not listed as the first author cannot be added.
          There are no “co-first authors”.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>How do the different costs work for Additional Conference/TVCG Papers?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          Non-students pay € 300 for each additional conference/TVCG paper
          contribution, whereas students pay € 250 for each additional
          conference/TVCG paper contribution.
        </li>

        <li>
          Restrictions apply to adding contributions as described above.
        </li>

        <li>
          The non-student/student costs are determined based on the registration
          upon which the contribution is being attached, not the contribution itself.
          That is, a non-student registrant attaching a “student paper” in which they
          are an author still pays € 300 for the additional contribution, not € 250.
          If a student registrant attaches that same paper, the cost is € 250
          (note the student must be first author for this to be valid).
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Who can present work at ISMAR?</summary>
    <div class="faq-content">
      <ul>
        <li>
          <u>All</u> ISMAR presenters must be registered with one of:

          <ul>
            <li>Student IEEE member/non-member Full Conference Author Registration</li>
            <li>Non-student IEEE member/non-member Full Conference Author Registration</li>
            <li>IEEE life member</li>
          </ul>

          <p>Note: Note: This policy applies to workshop paper, workshop poster and workshop demo authors as well.</p>
        </li>

        <li>
          As a general rule, each contribution must be presented by the registrant
          associated with that contribution. Special cases may be considered,
          provided that both the registrant of the contribution and the preferred
          presenter are fully registered, and the contribution is attached to a Full Conference Author registration.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </details>
