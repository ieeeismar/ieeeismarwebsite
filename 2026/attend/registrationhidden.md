## New Registration Fee Model

For ISMAR 2026, we are proudly offering a new registration fee model.  We offer this model after significant effort, thorough conversations with peers and the ISMAR Steering Committee. 

In short, there are two key aspects to the new registration fee model: 
1. Student authors can register as an IEEE Student Member or Student Non-Member;

2. All registering authors can attach one contribution in their base registration cost and add more contributions for an additional fee(s) as needed.  

More details about this new registration model are provided below.

## FAQs


**When do I need to select Author Registration?**

- You must select Author Registration if you are an author of any accepted contribution (conference paper, journal paper, invited TVCG paper, invited CG&A paper, workshop, workshop paper, poster, or research demo) and no other co-author/co-organizer has yet to complete an Author Registration (and attached the shared contribution). At least one Author Registration per contribution is mandatory for inclusion in the conference program and/or proceedings.

**I am invited to present a previously published TVCG or CG&A paper. Which registration category should I choose?**

- You should register under Author Registration.

**What information is required when registering as an invited TVCG or CG&A paper author?**

- You must provide the full Paper DOI, for example:

`10.1109/TVCG.20XX.XXXXXXX` (as listed in IEEE TVCG.)

**Do Doctoral Consortium presenters need an Author Registration?**

- No. Author Registration is not required if you are only presenting at the Doctoral Consortium.

- However, if you also have an accepted contribution – i.e., conference paper, journal paper, invited TVCG paper, workshop, poster, research demo, workshop paper, workshop poster, or workshop demo – at least one Author Registration is required for that contribution to be included in the conference program and/or proceedings.

**Can I register multiple times?**

- No. Each participant may complete only one registration.

**I am a Student Volunteer with a complimentary registration, but I am also an author. What should I do?**

Each paper requires an Author Registration. Either:

- A co-author completes the Author Registration for your paper, or
- You register yourself under Author Registration, even if you have a complimentary Student Volunteer registration.

Any additional contributions beyond the first must be paid separately according to the registration fee table above.

**What is the new registration fee model?**

- The new model sets a base cost for student and non-student IEEE members/non-members that includes one contribution of any kind.

- Student contributors register as student members/non-members and pay a lower base cost. Non-student contributors register as regular members/non-members and pay a higher base cost.

- Each registration includes, at no additional cost, one contribution.

- Additional contributions can be added in an à la carte style.

- Certain restrictions apply to these additional contributions, concomitant with the overall goals of the new model (see below for more details).

**What are the goals of the new registration fee model?**

In short, our goals for this new approach are, in order of priority:

1. **Reduce costs for contributing students:** Over the years, ISMAR (and other conferences) have moved to a model whereby every contributing author regardless of their career stage pay full conference, IEEE member/non-member fees. In these cases, a student presenting a poster would pay the same as a senior researcher presenting two papers.

2. **Encourage more student involvement** (in general) to grow the community and establish long-lasting quality cohorts. This means creating cost structures that encourage faculty and career researchers to bring students to ISMAR.

3. **Allow registrants to add an arbitrary number of contributions** to their registrations. This provides flexibility for students and faculty/career researchers alike. While we would prefer to see more student involvement, we know this is not possible for everyone in all cases.

**How does the current registration fee model compare to the recent 1+1 model?**

To answer this question, assume that there exists a registration fee structure that employs the recent 1+1 registration model, and generates the equivalent income as the new model. Using these fees as a baseline, we can confidently state that the new model:

- Reduces costs for student-authors with one contribution by 21%.
- Keeps costs for student-authors with 2 contributions approximately the same.
- Keeps costs for non-student authors with one contribution approximately the same.
- Increases costs for non-student authors with two contributions by 35%.

**What contributions must be attached to a registration?**

- For contributions to be included in the IEEE Digital Library they must be attached to a valid registration (see below for who can register and the restrictions on adding additional contributions).

- Contributions that must be attached include: TVCG papers, ISMAR conference papers, posters, demos, invited TVCG and invited CG&A papers, workshops, workshop papers, workshop posters, and workshop demos.

- At least one workshop organizer must attach their workshop as a contribution to their registration.

- Contributions should be attached only to a single registration. Do not attach contributions to multiple registrations! This is confusing for the organizers and can lead to delays in registration processing.

**Who can register contributions?**

For contributions to be included in the IEEE Digital Library all contributions must be registered under one of the following registration categories:

- Student IEEE member/non-member full conference registration
- Non-student IEEE member/non-member full conference registration
- IEEE life member

One-day passes cannot be used to register contributions.

**What restrictions apply when adding additional contributions?**

- Non-students can add as many contributions as they wish as long as the registrant is one of the listed authors on each contribution registered.

- Students can add any contributions in which their name appears first in the author list (traditionally termed “first author”). Contributions where a student is not listed as the first author cannot be added. There are no “co-first authors”.

**How do the different costs work for Additional Conference/TVCG Papers?**

- Non-students pay € 350 for each additional conference/TVCG paper contribution, whereas students pay € 300 for each additional conference/TVCG paper contribution.

- Restrictions apply to adding contributions as described above.

- The non-student/student costs are determined based on the registration upon which the contribution is being attached, not the contribution itself. That is, a non-student registrant attaching a “student paper” in which they are an author still pays € 350 for the additional contribution, not € 300. If a student registrant attaches that same paper, the cost is € 300 (note the student must be first author for this to be valid).

**Who can present work at ISMAR?**

- <u>All</u> ISMAR presenters must be registered with one of:

    - Student IEEE member/non-member full-conference registration
    - Non-student IEEE member/non-member full-conference registration
    - IEEE life member

   Note: This policy applies to workshop paper authors as well.

- As a general rule, each contribution must be presented by the registrant associated with that contribution. Special cases may be considered, provided that both the registrant of the contribution and the preferred presenter are fully registered.

**Why do registration costs seem higher than previous ISMAR conferences, independent of the registration model used?**

We have done our best to keep costs down, and comparable other conferences recently held in Europe (e.g., CHI 2026 Barcelona). Nonetheless, the following are issues we face while preparing for ISMAR 2026 in Italy:

- Costs for almost all goods and services are rising, and it is simply not possible to keep fees as they have been in 2024 and 2025.
- We want to deliver the quality experience the ISMAR community expects!
- Lunch will be included, as the surrounding area cannot effectively accommodate 600 people during lunchtime.
